About Us

In a world that rushes, we choose to pause. The Velvet Affairs is an ode to the art of slow celebration. Founded by Muskaan Jain and Yashika Goyal, our philosophy is rooted in a simple truth:
Grandeur is meaningless without Soul.

We are the storytellers of your joy. Muskaan brings the visual poetry—the aesthetics of palaces and the elegance of royalty. Yashika brings the emotional anchor—the sanctity of rituals and the warmth of hospitality. Together, we weave a tapestry where tradition meets modern finesse.

Whether it is the first giggle of your child or the silent prayer of a Havan, we believe every moment has a texture. We ensure that texture is as soft, as rich, and as timeless as velvet. We invite you to leave the logistics to us, so you can be free to live the moment, breathe in the joy, and be truly present with the ones you love.

The Velvet Vow


To us, you are not a client; you are family. We vow to treat your celebration with the same sanctity and care as we would our own. We promise that when you entrust us with your memories, we will handle them with 'Velvet Hands'—soft, careful, and luxurious. We vow to be invisible in our execution so that your joy remains the only visible spectacle. From the first marigold strung to the last lamp lit, our commitment is to honor your traditions, respect your emotions, and curate an experience that lingers in your heart long after the guests have gone. This is our promise. This is The Velvet Affairs.

Our Philosophy

We believe that a celebration is a living, breathing entity. It has a heartbeat. At The Velvet Affairs, our philosophy is simple: we place meaning above spectacle.

We vow to treat your memories with 'Velvet Hands'—gentle, reverent, and luxurious. When you entrust us with your occasion, you are not hiring a manager; you are adopting a family that worries about the flowers, the food, and the feelings so you don't have to.

We promise to be invisible in our execution but unmistakable in our impact. We vow to honor the ancient rhythms of India—the timing of the Aarti, the color of the Haldi, the sound of the Dhol—ensuring that your celebration echoes not just in photos, but in the hearts of those who witnessed it.
